A man looked through some boxes with his grandson. 
They were filled with old photographs and objects that **portrayed**^[To portray something means to describe it or show it in a picture.] important events from the grandfather's life. 
He wished to share the **circumstances**^[A circumstance is an event that makes a situation what it is.] behind each event with his grandson.

The grandson, however, thought his grandfather's stories were boring.
The grandfather **coped**^[To cope with a difficult or stressful situation means to deal with it.] with this.
He ignored his grandson's **criticism**^[Criticism is the act of saying that something is not correct or good.].

He took a photo from the box.
"That's the **submarine**^[tàu ngầm] I was on during the war,” 
he explained. 
The grandson **gazed**^[To gaze at something means to look at it for a long time.] at it.

The grandfather **glanced**^[To glance at something means to look at it quickly.] at the next picture and **frowned**^[To frown is to make an unhappy look with one's face.].
It showed a row of **coffins**^[A coffin is a box used to bury dead people.]. 
His grandson noticed the **grief**^[Grief is the feeling of deep sadness, usually when a person dies.] in his grandfather's face.

"What is it?" 
the boy inquired.

"This was after a **nuclear bomb**^[bom hạt nhân] was dropped,"
the grandfather answered. 
"It **devastated**^[To devastate something is to cause great damage or pain to that thing.] a city"

Next, the grandfather pulled a toy **microscope**^[kính hiển vi] from the box and rotated it in his hand.

"Where did you get that?”
the grandson asked.

"This is a **souvenir**^[A souvenir is something bought to remember of a place or event.] I bought at the science museum," 
the grandfather said.

Now, the boy was really interested.
He started to understand that his grandfather was telling him a larger story.
It was the story of his grandfather's life.

The grandfather picked up another photo. 
It showed a young **bride**^[cô dâu] and **groom**^[chú rể]. 
They were very happy. 
A certificate was attached to the photo. 
The boy couldn't read it, 
but he **traced**^[To trace something means to follow over it with the eyes or a finger.] his finger over the paper.

"What's this from, Granddad?" 
he asked.

"That's my marriage license from the day I married your grandmother,"
the grandfather said.

"Wow!" said the boy. 
"Granddad, now I know all about your life!"
